Problem Statement

How might we improve communication about household responsibilities for college students living together so that residents can better manage their finances and chores?

Project Overview

Throughout the quarter, our team embarked on an extensive design process to address the communication challenges among college students living together, focusing on managing household responsibilities and finances. Our approach was multifaceted, incorporating competitive analysis, user research, persona development, storyboarding, and the creation of prototypes at various levels of fidelity. Below, we detail the key components of our design products and how each contributed to the development of our final solution.

Competitive Analysis

Initially, we conducted a competitive analysis to understand the existing solutions in the market. This analysis helped us identify gaps in current offerings and opportunities for innovation. By examining the strengths and weaknesses of existing apps and tools, we were able to define unique value propositions for our own solution.

Mid-Fi Prototype

Building on the feedback received, we developed mid-fidelity prototypes, which introduced more detailed interactions and visual design elements. These prototypes were essential for conducting usability testing and refining our design.

Interactive Prototype

Finally, we created high-fidelity prototypes that closely resembled the final product. These prototypes provided a realistic representation of our solution, complete with branding, detailed UI elements, and interactive features. They were crucial for final user testing and stakeholder presentations.
